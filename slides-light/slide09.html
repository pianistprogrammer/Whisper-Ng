The Challenges

09 / 13
What makes Nigerian language speech recognition uniquely difficult
📉
Data Scarcity
The primary bottleneck
Nigerian Pidgin has 38 samples total. Yoruba and Igbo sit at ~2,400 each. Even Hausa — the best-resourced — has just 27K samples vs. millions for English. The model memorises instead of learning phonetic rules.
🎵
Tonal Languages & Diacritics
Yoruba · Igbo
Pitch changes word meaning entirely. Diacritics like ọ, ẹ, ṣ, á, à are routinely dropped by contributors typing on standard keyboards. The model outputs the correct form, gets penalised by WER for it.

🔄
Code-Switching
Mid-sentence language mixing
Nigerian speakers naturally alternate between their native language, Pidgin, and English within a single sentence. No training set captures this fluently. Tokeniser breaks down at language boundaries — hallucination loops, spelling collapse.
🗺️
Dialectal Variation
20+ dialects per language
Igbo and Yoruba each have 20+ regional dialects with distinct pronunciations and vocabulary. A model trained on Lagos Yoruba degrades significantly on Ibadan or Ekiti speech. No dialect-level benchmarks exist today.
📝
Orthographic Inconsistency
Community-contributed noise
The same word can be transcribed 5 different ways across the dataset. Normalisation risks stripping phonemic meaning. Evaluation metrics that punish correct-but-differently-spelled outputs paint a falsely grim picture.
💻
Compute Constraints
Democratising AI research
Full Whisper fine-tuning requires 40GB+ GPU. LoRA + 8-bit quantisation makes training possible on Apple Silicon — but MPS is 3–5× slower than an A100. African research labs rarely have data-centre access.
