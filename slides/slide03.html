Origin Story

A Personal App.
A Bigger Question.

It started simply — build a voice assistant I could actually talk to. JustTalk: a native macOS app powered by Whisper. It worked beautifully, in English.

"Can it understand me in Yoruba?"
"What about Hausa?"
— Friends and colleagues, repeatedly
That question didn't have a good answer. And the more I dug in, the more I realised: this wasn't a feature request — it was a research problem.
What The Research Revealed

The Philosophical Problem
OpenAI Whisper is trained on 680,000 hours of multilingual audio — yet Nigerian languages are nearly absent. If an AI can't understand you in your own language, it is not universally intelligent. It is selectively intelligent.
The Mission
Fine-tune Whisper to understand Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo, and Nigerian Pidgin — then open-source the work and build a community-driven data collection platform so the gap keeps closing.
